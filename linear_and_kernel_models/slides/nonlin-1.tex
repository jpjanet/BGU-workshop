

What about non-linear regression?\pause{} Let's say we want to use a quadratic model  and assume a 2D input variable:
\begin{align*}
\only<2>{y_{QUAD}(x) =  w_1 + w_2x_1 + w_3 x_2 +w_4 x_1x_2 + w_5  x_{1}^2  + w_6 x_{2}^{2}}
\only<3->{y_{QUAD}(x) =  w_1 + w_2\sqrt{2}x_1 + w_3 \sqrt{2}x_2 +w_4\sqrt{2} x_1x_2 + w_5  x_{1}^2  + w_6 x_{2}^{2}}
\end{align*}
\visible<4->{Notice that this is \textit{linear} in $w$ for a `lifted' feature space, $\varphi(X)$:
\begin{align*}
 y_{QUAD}(x) &=     \varphi(X) w \\
 &= \begin{footnotesize}\begin{bmatrix}
1 & \sqrt{2}x_1^{(1)} & \sqrt{2}x_2^{(1)} & \sqrt{2}x_1^{(1)} x_2^{(1)} & (x_1^{(1)})^{2} & (x_{2}^{(1)})^{2} \\ 
\vdots   & & \vdots\\
1 & \sqrt{2}x_1^{(n)} & \sqrt{2}x_2^{(n)} & \sqrt{2}x_1^{(1)} x_2^{(n)} &(x_1^{(n)})^{2} & (x_{2}^{(n)})^{2} \\ 
\end{bmatrix}  
\begin{bmatrix}
w_1 \\ \vdots \\ w_6
\end{bmatrix} \end{footnotesize}
\end{align*}}
\visible<4->{
except the dimension has increased from $\mathbb{R}^{n\times2} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{n\times6}$}
